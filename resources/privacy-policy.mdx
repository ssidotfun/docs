**Effective:** May 2025

**1. Introduction**

SSI.FUN ("SSI", "we", "our", or "us") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our no-code Web3 platform that transforms ideas into tokenized applications with integrated marketing and AI workflows.

**2. Information We Collect**

**2.1 Personal Information**

- **Account Registration Data:** Name, email address, username, wallet address.
- **Profile Information:** Bio, avatar, social media links.

**2.2 Usage Data**

- **Platform Activity:** Pages visited, features used, transactions, tokens created, and contributions made.
- **Communications:** Messages, feedback, support inquiries.

**2.3 Technical Data**

- **Device & Log Data:** IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers, crash reports.
- **Cookies & Tracking:** Session cookies, analytics cookies, and performance tags.

**3. How We Use Your Information**

We use your data to:

1. **Operate & Improve Services:** Provide, maintain, and enhance platform features.
2. **Account Management:** Authenticate and secure your account, and communicate updates or support.
3. **Community & Ecosystem:** Facilitate interactions among creators, users, investors, and influencers.
4. **Marketing & Analytics:** Send promotional materials (with consent), analyze usage trends, and optimize user experience.
5. **Compliance:** Meet legal and regulatory obligations.

**4. Sharing & Disclosure**

We may share your information with:

- **Service Providers:** Third-party vendors that help with hosting, analytics, payment processing, and customer support.
- **Partners:** SSI Foundation, Swarms Foundation, and approved collaborators within our ecosystem.
- **Legal Authorities:** When required by law or to protect our rights and users.

**5. Data Security**

We implement industry-standard measures to protect your data, including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, and regular security assessments. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.
